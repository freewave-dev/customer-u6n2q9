Our Story

An original homestead, lovingly kept.

Tucked into the rolling hills of San Juan Island, our home is one of the original homesteads on the island — built in the 1880s and quietly cared for ever since. The floors creak a little. The light through the kitchen window is the same it was a century ago.

What was once a working farmstead is now a place for travelers to land softly. The main house is ours; the detached cottage out back is entirely yours — a small, private world set apart by garden and quiet.

And when you arrive, you'll find a warm loaf of Debbie's homemade bread waiting on the kitchen counter. It's a small thing, and it's the whole point.

— Debbie & the homestead

The Cottage

A private guest house, set apart from the main home.

The cottage sits a short stroll from the main house — close enough for a fresh loaf of bread, far enough for a true escape. It sleeps two comfortably, with thoughtful period touches and modern comforts where they matter most.

  • Queen bed with linen sheets & a wool blanket for cool island nights
  • Private bath with a deep clawfoot tub and walk-in shower
  • Kitchenette with kettle, drip coffee, and a small cast-iron stove
  • Wood-floored porch with two chairs and a view of the garden
  • Books, blankets, and a record player — no televisions
  • Fresh-cut flowers and that loaf of bread on arrival

Outside Your Door

San Juan Island, at the pace it deserves.

From orca pods in the Salish Sea to a working lavender farm down the road, Friday Harbor is the rare town that hasn't lost its softness. Here's how guests tend to spend their days.

01

Whale Watching

Boats leave from Friday Harbor each morning — resident orca pods, humpbacks, harbor seals, and bald eagles overhead.

02

Friday Harbor Town

A four-minute drive: independent shops, dock-fresh seafood, a great bookstore, and the ferry's coming and going.

03

Lavender & Lighthouses

Pelindaba Lavender Farm in summer; Lime Kiln Point in any season, where the orcas come closest to shore.

04

Slow Back Roads

Rent bikes or drive the long way home. The island is small enough to know in a weekend, and large enough to keep surprising you.
